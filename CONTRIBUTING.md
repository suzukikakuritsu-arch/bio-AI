# Contributing to bio-AI

First off, thank you for showing interest in the **IET-SYNC (Information Emergence Theory)** framework and our graded auxetic research. To ensure the integrity of the "Suzuki Absolute Principle" and maintain high-quality engineering standards, please follow these guidelines.

## How Can I Contribute?

### 1. Reporting Bugs
If you find any inconsistencies in the lattice generation or nodal synchronization, please open an **Issue**. 
* Provide a clear description of the parameter set used (e.g., base_width, k_factor).
* Explain the expected vs. actual structural outcome.

### 2. Suggesting Enhancements
Ideas for new unit cell geometries or optimization algorithms are welcome. 
* Please open an Issue first to discuss the theoretical grounding (alignment with IET principles).
* This ensures that new features remain consistent with the project's core "Resonance Keys."

### 3. Pull Requests (PRs)
When you are ready to contribute code:
1.  **Fork the repository** to your own account.
2.  **Create a branch** for your feature or fix.
3.  **Ensure code quality**: Follow the existing style (clean, modular, and documented).
4.  **Reference the Origin**: Any derivative works must acknowledge the foundational logic developed by Suzuki Yukiya.

## Our Standards

* **Intellectual Integrity**: Do not attempt to claim the core mathematical models (specifically the interlocking and ramp function logic) as original discovery. Attribution is mandatory.
* **Academic Rigor**: Contributions should ideally be backed by biomedical engineering principles or structural mechanics data.
* **Respectful Collaboration**: As this is a global research effort, maintain professional communication in all Issues and PRs.

## Attribution Policy
By contributing to this repository, you agree that your contributions will be licensed under the project's MIT License. The primary architect remains **Suzuki Yukiya**, and your name will be added to the `CONTRIBUTORS` list upon successful integration of your code.
